# Product

## Register

product

## Users

FS PBX is used by a mixed audience with varied technical skill: PBX administrators, MSP and tenant operators, support staff, and people who need to complete phone-system tasks without deep telephony knowledge. Users are often configuring operational systems, troubleshooting live behavior, managing customer domains, or responding to time-sensitive support needs.

## Product Purpose

FS PBX is a dense Laravel, Vue, Inertia, VueForm, and FreeSWITCH admin application for managing modern PBX operations. It should make complex telephony workflows understandable, efficient, and dependable while preserving the power needed by advanced administrators. Success means users can find the right surface quickly, make confident changes, and understand system state without needing legacy FusionPBX context.

## Brand Personality

Modern, calm, elegant.

The interface should feel capable and trustworthy without becoming cold or difficult. It should make difficult PBX work feel organized, approachable, and controlled.

## Anti-references

Do not recreate legacy FusionPBX clutter, dense visual noise, scattered actions, ambiguous form layouts, or pages that require prior tribal knowledge to operate. Avoid marketing-style admin UI, decorative excess, oversized hero sections, and generic SaaS gloss that gets in the way of repeated operational work.

## Design Principles

- Follow proven app patterns first: Devices, Ring Grouos, Extension, Default Settings pages, VueForm conventions, and existing Laravel/Inertia data-route structure.
- Make dense workflows easy to scan: prioritize clear hierarchy, predictable placement, compact controls, and useful table structure.
- Support mixed skill levels: expose expert power while keeping labels, states, and actions plain enough for less technical users.
- Preserve operational calm: status, errors, destructive actions, and live-system controls should be obvious without feeling alarming.
- Refine away legacy clutter: every migrated or modernized surface should feel simpler, cleaner, and more intentional than the old PHP UI.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA where practical. Keep keyboard navigation, visible focus states, color-blind-safe status cues, reduced-motion tolerance, and readable contrast in mind for all operational screens. Do not rely on color alone for critical status or destructive actions.
